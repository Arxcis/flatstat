🏅 achievements 💥 holes

🏅 Achievement: --wayland-with-fallback

Your app supports Wayland. Wayland supports isolation between apps. If the user is in a Wayland session, your app does not have access to x11. At the same time your app remains backwards-compatible with x11-users, giving x11-users access to the Flatpak ecosystem.

🏅 Achievement: --no-filesystem-all

Your app does not ask for blanket access to the entire filesystem, like --filesystem=home or --filesystem=host.

🏅 Achievement: --no-filesystem-unknown

Your app does not ask for access to any unknown files or directories - outside of the "well-known directories" like xdg-download, xdg-music, etc.

🏅 Achievement: --no-filesystem

Your app does not ask for blanket access to any files or directories, leaving the user in full control of which files the app is given access to. Your app is likely using the org.freedsektop.portal.FileChooser-portal.

🏅 Achievement: --no-device-all

Your app does not ask for blanket access to all of the users connected devices, aka --device=all.

🏅 Achievement: --no-device

Your app does not ask for blanket access to any connected devices, leaving the user in full control of which devices the app is given access to. Your app is likely using portals instead.

🏅 Achievement: --no-pulseaudio

Your app does not ask for blanket access to the users audio.

🏅 Achievement: --no-name-talk

Your app does not talk to any names on the system- or session-bus. Your app is likely using portals instead.

🏅 Achievement: --no-name-own

Your app does not own any names on the system- or session-bus. Your app is likely using portals instead.

💥 Hole: --x11

Your app supports x11. x11 does not support isolation between apps, which enables your app to see what a user is doing in other apps. E.g. keylogging, screen capture.

💥 Hole: --filesystem-all

Your app has blanket access to all of the user's private files, because it is using permissions like --filesystem=home or --filesystem=host

💥 Hole: --filesystem-unknown

Your app has blanket access to one or more unknown files or directories - outside of "well-known directories" like xdg-desktop, xdg-music, etc.

💥 Hole: --filesystem-well-known

Your app has blanket access to one or more of the well-known directories - like xdg-desktop, xdg-music, etc. This is better than having access to unknown files or directories, or access to all files or directories, but is still considred a hole, because there is no isolation between apps.

💥 Hole: --device-all

Your app has blanket access to all of the user's connected devices.

💥 Hole: --device-well-known

Your app has blanket access to one or more of the user's "well-known devices", like --device=dri, or --device=kvm.

💥 Hole: --pulseaudio

Your app has pulseaudio access. Pulseaudio does not support isolation between app. Your app can listen to the microphone without asking the user. Your app can play sound without asking the user.

💥 Hole: --talks-session-name

Your app has declared that it is talking to one or more names on the session-bus, using: --talk-name=NAME

💥 Hole: --talks-system-name

Your app has declared that it is talking to one or more names on the system-bus, using: --system-talk-name=NAME

💥 Hole: --owns-session-name

Your app has declared that it owns one or more names on the session-bus, using: --own-name=NAME

💥 Hole: --owns-system-name

Your app has declared that it owns one or more names on the system-bus, using: --system-own-name=NAME
